Current airframes are majority composite, this is due to the fact that using composites you can reduce the structural mass of the aircraft and make it more efficient. 

For every kg reduction in aircraft mass it produces around 372kg less CO2 every year.

Over recent years we have seen the OEW as a fraction of MTOW decrease:
OEW/MTOW for A380 - 0.54
OEW/MTOW for A350 - 0.45

The issue with this is the fact that the majority of composites are non-recyclable and therefore are bad for the environment.

If we continue to adopt composites which are stronger & utilise other methods, such as new structural concepts, reducing the Factor of Safety using better load estimation and better manufacturing control and implementation of structural health monitoring we could reduce the overall amount of composite required and ease issues with recycling in that respect.

### Composite Materials
Current polymer matrix composites use thermoset resins which are not recyclable and artificial fibres, which have very high carbon footprints. Thermoplastics are an alternative which can be easily recycled, however there is a high carbon footprint for manufacturing.

### Energy Optimisation
- **Carbon Fibre Manufacturing Process**
	- Short term
		- More efficient furnaces
		- Waste energy recovery
	- Long term
		- Targeted heating
		- Recycling fibres as precursors
		- Less energy intensive precursors like polyolefins

- **Other Possibilities**
	- Alternate OoA (out of autoclaive) processes without prepregs
	- Alternate resin systems like thermoplastics
	- Distributed manufacturing & localised supply chains
	- Recycled carbon fibres

Advantages:
- Out of plane properties
- Formability
- Reduced takt time
- Z-directional thermal conductivity

Properties of 3D composites
- In plane properties (tensile, compressive, shear)
	- Various types of defects are inadvertently created during the 3D weaving/braiding/stitching/pinning process
	- Similar of slightly higher than an equivalent 2D material
- Out of plane properties (CAI, bird strike, FOD etc)
	- Significantly improved, with minimal compromise in in-plane properties
		- Damage assessment of NCF, 2D and 3D woven composites under compression after multiple-impact using C-scan, X-ray, CAI & acoustic emission.
