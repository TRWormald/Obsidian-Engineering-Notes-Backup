## Other Decarbonisation Options
### Turboprops
Turboprops are very efficient, due to the fact that they are unshrouded and therefore have a very high BPR. However they have high tip losses in the transonic regime so they are excellent at low Mach numbers.

They also have challenges with:
- Unsteady aerodynamic interactions
- Noise and Vibrations
- System complexity, and high weight
- Stability issues

### Distributed Propulsion
Wingtip propellers can reduce the lift induced drag of an aircraft. This is due to the fact that they rotate opposite to the wing tip vortices and introduce swirl leading to upwash on wing.

Leading edge propellers can increase both lift induced drag and $D_{0}$ this is done through reduced wing size and weight. They also have an advantage of reduced noise if you synchro-phase their propellers.

### Electric Propulsion Efficiencies

It is possible to have efficiencies as high as 100%. However there are problems:
- Battery weight - typically 40x times less energy dense than jet fuel
- Battery stack leading to 10% additional weight
- Residual charge after current is below the critical level
- Performance decreases in low temperatures

## Alternate Fuel Systems
### Conventional Aviation Fuel
There are 3 main fuels:
- Jet A1 - suitable for flying at high altitudes (freezing point of -47)
- Jet A - similar to Jet A1 but with a worse freezing point
- Jet B - fuel with a low freezing point (-76 degrees)

They are all refined kerosine.
