### Aircraft Force Balance
In cruise:
$$W=L=\frac{1}{2}\rho V^{2}S C_{L}$$
$$T=D=\frac{1}{2}\rho V^{2}S C_{D}$$
### Aerodynamic Efficiency Index
We can calculate Aerodynamic Efficiency to be:
$$\eta_{aero}=\frac{Lift}{Drag}=\frac{C_{L}}{C_{D}}$$
Therefore if we want to increase the aerodynamic efficiency we either need to increase the $C_{L}$, decrease the $C_{D}$ or both.

#### Reducing Drag
We can decrease the sources of drag in different ways:

| Reducing $D_0$                        | Reducing $D_L$                         |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Streamlined configuration             | Increase Aspect Ratio                  |
| Smaller LGs, conformal antennae       | Increase $b_{eff}$ for the same L or W |
| Smooth surface finish                 |                                        |
| Low $S_{wet}$                         |                                        |
| Technologies to delay flow separation |                                        |
Note that $b_{eff}$ is the effective span i.e. $span+winglets$. So:
$$D_{L}=(L/b_{eff})^{2}=(W/b_{eff})^{2}$$
### Novel Aerodynamics to Reduce Drag
#### Boundary Layer Control
Managing the thin layer of air clinging to the a/c can significantly reduce drag. This can be done using:
- Hybrid laminar flow control (HLCF), which involves actively sucking in slow moving boundary air to reduce drag.
	- This can result in fuel savings of up to 15%
- Coatings & Materials: developing surfaces that reduce friction effectively

#### Natural Laminar Flow
This is a passive method for shape optimisation to maintain smoother airflow & reduce drag. E.g:
- HondaJet
- The X-59

#### Riblets & Surface Textures
- Bio-mimicry: inspired by sharkskin
- Tiny grooves on an aircraft's surface (moulded into a plastic film with an adhesive backing) to reduce drag by controlling airflow.
- Airbus tested it on the A350 which saw a 1% fuel saving.

### Novel Aircraft Configurations
#### Truss Based Wing
Using a truss support you can increase the AR of a wing, without increasing structural mass significantly. This reduces lift induced drag, and sees a reduction in fuel burn of 10%.

#### Blended Wing (non-military)
The motivations for this are to decrease $S_{wet}$ for a given volume and have minimum surface discontinuities. Winglets are typically included to increase $b_{eff}$. It also distributes the weight of the aircraft and hence lowers the required structural weight.
