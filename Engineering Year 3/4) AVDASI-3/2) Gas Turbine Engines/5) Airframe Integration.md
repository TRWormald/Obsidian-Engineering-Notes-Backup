### Introduction
#### Air Vehicle Performance
To calculate Air Vehicle Performance (in steady 1 'g' flight) the following are needed:
- Drag & mass of the aircraft (usually in the form of drag polars & weight breakdowns)
- Engine thrust & fuel flow characteristics
Combining the drag polars with engine performance gives the Air Vehicle Performance.

#### The Effect of the Engine on the Airframe
Intake flow:
- Flow into the intake can effect the fuselage and wing aerodynamics
Exhaust flow:
- Scrubbing over fuselage can effect drag & give rise to structural problems
- Exhaust flow at certain values of incidence can cause instabilities & fatigue problems on Fin & Tailplane
- Propeller slipstreams can have a significant effect on both wing & tailplane aerodynamics and on the airframe structural design due to the levels of noise and vibration.

#### The Effect on the Airframe on the Engines
Engine is the source of secondary power & bleed air:
- Power to run systems
- Air for cabin conditioning and cooling etc.

Nozzles:
- Special nozzles can effect engine performance
- Vectoring nozzles may be needed for manoeuvring and STOVL
